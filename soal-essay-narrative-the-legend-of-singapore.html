THE LEGEND OF SINGAPORE

Long ago, Sang Nila Utama, the prince of Sumatra, wanted a better place for a new city. He tried very hard to look for it. Once, he spotted a deer at a nearby island and started chasing it. He came to a very large rock and climbed it. When he reached the top, he looked across the sea and saw another island with a white sandy beach.

One of his ministers told that it was the island of Temasek. Taking the risk, he sailed across the sea quickly to Temasek. However, when his ship as out at sea, a great strom blew up and the ship was tossed about in the huge waves. It began to take water. To prevent ship from sinking, his man threw all the heavy things on board into the sea to lighten the ship. Strangely, the storm died down after he threw his crown overboard. Then he reached Temasek safely.

After landing at Temasek, he went inland to hunt wild animals. Suddenly, he saw a strange animal with a red body, black head and a  white breast. It was a fine-looking animal and moved with great speed as it disappeared into the jungle.

His chief minister told that it probably was a lion. He was pleased with this as he believed it to be a good omen - a sign of good fortune coming his way. Thus, he started deveoping his new city in Temasek. After working very hard day and night, a new city was found. He named this city SINGAPURA. SINGA means lion and PURA means city.

1. From the story we know that...

a. Sang Nila Utama was a prince of Temasek.

b. Sang Nila Utama saw a strange animal at a nearby island

c. The minister told that a strange animal was a deer.

d. Sang Nila Utama saw a strange animal at Temasek.

Answer : D

2. What did the men do to lighten the ship?

a. They prevented the ship from sinking.

b. They plunged the crown into the sea.

c. They threw heavy things overboard.

d. They sailed across the sea quickly.

Answer : C

3. Based on the text we can conclude that ..

a. The city was built through hard work and perseverance

b. Nila Utama chased a fine-looking animal into the jungle.

c. The prince saw a deer in the island of Temasek.

d. They took water to prevent ship from sinking.

Answer : A

4. What is the main idea of the last paragraph?

a. The prince believed that the lion was a good omen.

b. The minister named the new founded city Singapura.

c. Singapura is derived from the word "singa" and "pura"

d. The prince and his men work all the time to build the city.

5. What can we learn from the story?

a. A lion is a good omen.

b. All hard work brings a profit.

c. There is no substitute for hard work.

d. A bad beginning, will make a bad end.

1. Narrative text is a text that tells about sequence of events in the past.

2. The purpose of narrative text is to entertain the reader with the story which can be fiction or non-fiction.

3. There are three generic structure of narrative text, namely: Orientation, Complication, and Resolution.

4. Language of narrative text:

– Past tense (killed, drunk, etc)

– Adverb of time (Once upun a time, one day, etc)

– Time conjunction (when, then, suddenly, etc)

– Specific character. The character of the story is specific, not general. (Cinderella, Snow White, Alibaba, etc)

– Action verbs. A verb that shows an action. (killed, dug, walked, etc)

– Direct speech. It is to make the story lively. (Snow White said,”My name is Snow White). The direct speech uses present tense.

Narrative Text

Narrative text adalah teks yang bertujuan untuk menghibur pembacanya. Terdapat beberapa jenis narrative text, yaitu:

1. Fable

2. Legend

3. Fairy tale

4. Horror

5. Romance

6. Mystery

7. Myth

8. dan lain lain

Generic Structure dari Narrative Text adalah:

• Orientation : It is about the opening paragraph where the characters of the story are introduced.(berisi pengenalan tokoh, tempat dan waktu terjadinya cerita (siapa atau apa, kapan dan dimana)

• Complication : Where the problems in the story developed. (Permasalahan muncul / mulai terjadi dan berkembang)

• Resolution : Where the problems in the story is solved. Masalah selesai, secara baik “happy ending” ataupun buruk “bad ending”.

• Coda / reorientation (optional) – lesson from the story

Language Features dari Narrative Text adalah:

• Use Past tense (killed, drunk, etc)

• Use adverb of time (Once upun a time, one day, etc)

• Use time conjunction (when, then, suddenly, etc)

• Use specific character. The character of the story is specific, not general. (Cinderella, Snow White, Alibaba, etc)

• Use action verbs. A verb that shows an action. (killed, dug, walked, etc)

• Use direct speech. It is to make the story lively. (Snow White said,”My name is Snow White). The direct speech uses present tense.

1. Description about myself:

Hello, my name is [Your Name]. I am a [Your Age]-year-old [Your Occupation/Student]. I have a passion for [Your Interests/Hobbies]. I am known for my [Positive Traits], such as being [Adjective 1] and [Adjective 2]. I believe in [Your Beliefs/Values] and strive to make a positive impact in [Your Community/Field of Interest]. In my free time, I enjoy [Activities you enjoy]. Overall, I am a [Adjective 3] individual who is constantly seeking growth and learning opportunities.

2. The social function of Narrative text:

The social function of narrative text is to entertain and engage the readers or listeners by telling a story. It aims to provide enjoyment, emotional connection, and a sense of escape from reality. Narrative texts often involve characters, settings, conflicts, and resolutions that take the readers on a journey through a sequence of events. Additionally, narrative texts can also convey moral lessons, cultural values, or social messages, depending on the purpose and context of the story.

3. Dialogue of introducing self:

Person A: Hi, I don't believe we've met before. I'm [Your Name]. What's your name?

Person B: Nice to meet you, [Your Name]. I'm [Other Person's Name]. Are you new here?

Person A: No, I've been here for a while. I work/study [Your Occupation/Field of Study]. How about you?

Person B: I'm a [Other Person's Occupation/Field of Study]. What do you like to do in your free time?

Person A: I enjoy [Your Hobbies/Interests], and I'm also passionate about [Other Topics/Activities]. How about you?

Person B: That sounds interesting! I'm into [Other Person's Hobbies/Interests] as well. We should definitely hang out sometime!

4. Some kinds of narrative text:

- Folktales: Traditional stories passed down through generations, often with cultural or moral lessons.

- Fairy tales: Imaginative stories featuring magical elements, enchanting settings, and often end with a happy ending.

- Legends: Stories based on real or fictional characters, often with supernatural or heroic elements.

- Myths: Traditional stories that explain natural phenomena, religious beliefs, or historical events.

- Fables: Short stories with anthropomorphic animals as characters, typically teaching moral lessons.

- Short stories: Fictional narratives that can stand alone and focus on a specific event, character, or theme.

- Novels: Longer works of fiction that explore complex plots, characters, and themes.

5. Generic structure of narrative text:

- Orientation: Introduces the setting, characters, and provides background information to set the scene.

- Complication: Introduces the conflict or problem faced by the characters, creating tension and driving the story forward.

- Resolution: Presents the climax and resolution of the conflict, where the problem is resolved or addressed.

- Reorientation (optional): Provides a conclusion or closing remarks, giving a sense of closure to the story.

- Coda (optional): Additional section that reflects on the events or offers an epilogue to wrap up the narrative.
